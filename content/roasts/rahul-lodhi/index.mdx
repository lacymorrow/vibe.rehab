## Wait - Is That a Leaked Secret?

Let's start with the scary one.

Our scanner flagged what it *thinks* might be a **LinkedIn Client Secret** sitting in a JavaScript bundle (`_next/static/chunks/aa30077ba7b2601e.js`). The matched pattern: `rrahul******2006`.

Now - could be a false positive. It looks like it could just be a username or ID that matches a credential pattern. But *if* it's a real OAuth client secret, it absolutely should not be in a frontend bundle. Despite the confusing name, "client secrets" are server-only credentials - the "client" refers to your *app* being a client of the OAuth provider, not the browser. Exposing one means anyone can impersonate your application.

Rahul - check this one first. If it's just a username, you're fine. If it's real, rotate it *today* and move it to a server-side environment variable.

## SEO: Nobody Can Find You

**45/100.** Here's why no recruiter is discovering this site through Google:

**The title tag is 20 characters.** The minimum for decent SEO is 30. "r2hu1 (Rahul Rajput)" is your GitHub handle, not a page title. It should be something like "Rahul Rajput - Full-Stack Developer & Open Source Contributor."

**The meta description is 388 characters.** Google shows 160 max. You wrote an entire paragraph where one sentence would've done the job. Google will just chop it off mid-sentence.

**Two H1 tags.** Pick one. Your name or your primary value prop. Everything else is H2 and below.

**No canonical URL**, so Google might index www and non-www as separate pages - splitting your already thin SEO juice in half.

The heading hierarchy also jumps from H1 straight to H3, skipping H2 entirely. That tells search engines your content structure is broken, and it tells screen readers the same thing.

## The Social Links Problem

This is the one that really got me. Rahul has 5 social links - Twitter, Discord, GitHub, LinkedIn, Instagram - and **every single one is just an icon with no accessible text.**

No `aria-label`. No visible text. No `title` attribute. A screen reader user hits those links and hears: "link." "link." "link." "link." "link." Five links in a row with zero information about where they go.

The fix is 30 seconds per link: `aria-label="Follow me on GitHub"`. That's it. Five links, two and a half minutes, biggest accessibility win on the entire site.

Those same links are also missing `rel="noopener"` - a security best practice for external links in new tabs. Without it, the destination page can access your `window.opener` object.

Then there's the 10 color contrast issues. That `text-muted-foreground` class looks sleek but "muted" and "accessible" are usually opposites. The light gray on dark background fails WCAG contrast ratios for a lot of users.

Oh, and two `<main>` landmarks on one page. Assistive tech expects exactly one. You've got `main.px-4` and `main.min-h-screen` both fighting for the role.

## Content: Thin but Honest

203 words on the entire homepage. Google generally wants 300+ to consider a page "content-rich." You don't need a novel, but a short paragraph about who you are, what you specialize in, and what you're looking for would make a real difference.

The LinkedIn social link also returns a 999 status code - LinkedIn's way of blocking automated requests. Automated tools (including Google) can't verify the link works. Consider using a proper LinkedIn vanity URL.

## The Fix List

Here's what I'd do, in order:

1. **Check that leaked secret** - if it's real, rotate it today and audit your env vars.
2. **Add `aria-label` to all 5 social links** - 30 seconds each, biggest accessibility win possible.
3. **Fix the title tag** - expand to 50-60 chars. Make it your name + what you do.
4. **Trim the meta description** - cut to 150-160 chars. One compelling sentence.
5. **Consolidate to one H1, one `<main>`** - fix the document structure.
6. **Add `rel="noopener"` to external links** - security hygiene.
7. **Add a canonical URL** - point www and non-www to the same place.
8. **Bump up contrast** - increase `text-muted-foreground` a shade or two.
9. **Add 100 more words of content** - who you are, what you do, what you're looking for.
10. **Add a charset declaration** - `<meta charset="utf-8">` in your head.

**Estimated fix time:** One afternoon. Maybe two hours.
**Priority:** High on the security check, medium on everything else
