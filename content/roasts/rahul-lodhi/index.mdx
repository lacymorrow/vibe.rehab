# Rahul Rajput's Portfolio: Great Dev, Rough Website

**Submitted by:** Rahul Rajput (yes, he asked for this)
**The site:** rahul.eu.org
**Score: 45/100 - Grade F**

## First Impressions

The site looks good. Dark theme, clean layout, real projects, active GitHub contribution graph lighting up the page like a heatmap of someone who actually ships code. shadcn-cookie-consent has 273 stars. ration, cavio, MySocials, MusicHub, IDE. All real. All linked. The "Resume" and "Let's Talk" buttons are right there.

Rahul is obviously a capable developer. That's what makes the rest of this so annoying.

## The Possible Leaked Secret

Our scanner flagged a pattern in `_next/static/chunks/aa30077ba7b2601e.js` that looks like a LinkedIn Client Secret: `rrahul******2006`.

Probably a false positive. Looks like a username that matches a credential pattern. But if it *is* a real OAuth secret in a client-side bundle, that's bad. "Client secret" is a misleading name. The "client" means your app, not the browser. It belongs on a server, never in frontend JavaScript.

Rahul: check this first. If it's a username, ignore it. If it's real, rotate it today.

## SEO

The title tag is 20 characters: "r2hu1 (Rahul Rajput)." That's a GitHub handle, not a page title. Google wants 30-60 characters. Something like "Rahul Rajput / Full-Stack Developer" would work.

The meta description is 388 characters. Google shows 160. Everything after that gets cut off mid-sentence.

Two H1 tags on one page. Heading hierarchy jumps from H1 to H3, skipping H2. No canonical URL, so Google might index the www and non-www versions as separate pages competing against each other.

203 words total on the homepage. Google generally treats anything under 300 as thin content. A short paragraph about what you specialize in and what you're looking for would push you past that threshold.

## Five Silent Links

Twitter. Discord. GitHub. LinkedIn. Instagram. Five social icons in the top right corner. Every one is just an SVG inside an anchor tag. No `aria-label`. No visible text. No `title` attribute.

A screen reader hits those and says: "link." "link." "link." "link." "link."

Where do they go? Nobody knows. The fix is `aria-label="GitHub"` on each one. Two minutes of work for the biggest accessibility win on the site.

Those same links are also missing `rel="noopener"`. Without it, the target page can access `window.opener` on your site. Low risk for most pages, but free to fix.

The LinkedIn URL returns a 999 status. That's LinkedIn blocking automated requests. Google can't verify the link works either.

Ten color contrast failures, mostly from `text-muted-foreground`. Muted and accessible don't usually go together. Bump that gray up a notch.

Two `<main>` landmarks on a single page: `main.px-4` and `main.min-h-screen`. Screen readers expect one.

## Fixes

1. **Verify the flagged secret.** If real, rotate it immediately.
2. **Add `aria-label` to all five social links.** Two minutes.
3. **Rewrite the title tag.** 50-60 characters. Name + role.
4. **Cut the meta description to 160 characters.** One good sentence.
5. **One H1. One `<main>`.** Fix the document structure.
6. **Add `rel="noopener"` to external links.**
7. **Set a canonical URL.**
8. **Increase contrast on muted text.**
9. **Add ~100 more words of content.**
10. **Add `<meta charset="utf-8">`.**

**Time estimate:** An afternoon. Two hours if you're focused.

## Verdict

45/100. A skilled developer's portfolio that's undermining itself with fixable mistakes. The projects speak for themselves. The site just needs to stop getting in their way.

Every issue on this list is a quick fix. The security flag is the only one with any urgency. Everything else is an afternoon of cleanup that'd push this into the 70s.
