## 🚨 Security: We Found Something

Let's start with the scariest finding:

<Issue type="warning">
Our scanner flagged what it *thinks* might be a **LinkedIn Client Secret** in a JavaScript bundle (`_next/static/chunks/aa30077ba7b2601e.js`). The matched pattern: `rrahul******2006`.

Now — this could be a false positive. It looks like it could just be a username or ID that happens to match a credential pattern. But if it IS a real OAuth client secret, it shouldn't be in a frontend bundle. Despite the confusing name, "client secrets" are server-only credentials — the "client" refers to your *app* being a client of the OAuth provider, not the browser. Exposing one lets anyone impersonate your application.

Rahul — worth a quick check. If it's just a username, no sweat. If it's an actual secret, rotate it and move it to a server-side environment variable.
</Issue>

## SEO: The Silent Killer

**Score: 45/100 — Grade F.** Here's why no recruiter is finding this site through Google:

**The title tag is 20 characters.** The minimum for proper SEO is 30. "r2hu1 (Rahul Rajput)" is your GitHub handle, not a page title. It should be something like "Rahul Rajput — Full-Stack Developer & Open Source Contributor."

**The meta description is 388 characters.** The maximum Google displays is 160. You wrote an essay where a sentence was needed. Google will truncate it and it'll look broken in search results.

**Two H1 tags on one page.** Pick one. The main heading should be your name or primary value proposition. Everything else is H2 and below.

**No canonical URL.** Google might index the www and non-www versions as separate pages, splitting your already thin SEO juice.

**No charset declaration.** Browsers will guess the encoding. They usually guess right. But "usually" isn't a spec.

<Issue type="warning">
The heading hierarchy jumps from H1 straight to H3, skipping H2 entirely. This tells search engines (and screen readers) that your content structure is broken.
</Issue>

## Accessibility: The Social Links Problem

This is the biggest UX failure on the site. Rahul has 5 social links — Twitter, Discord, GitHub, LinkedIn, Instagram — and **every single one is an icon with no accessible text.**

<Issue type="error">
Screen readers announce these links as... nothing. No `aria-label`, no visible text, no `title` attribute. A blind user navigating your portfolio encounters 5 links in a row that all say "link" with no indication of where they go.

The fix takes 30 seconds per link: `aria-label="Follow me on GitHub"`. That's it.
</Issue>

The same links also lack `rel="noopener"` — a security best practice for external links that open in new tabs. Without it, the destination page can access your `window.opener` object.

**10 color contrast issues** round out the accessibility problems. That trendy `text-muted-foreground` class? Turns out "muted" and "accessible" are often opposites. The light gray text on a dark background looks sleek but fails WCAG contrast ratios for many users.

Two `<main>` landmarks on one page is also a problem — assistive tech expects exactly one. You've got `main.px-4` and `main.min-h-screen` fighting for the role.

## Content: Thin but Honest

203 words on the entire homepage. The minimum for Google to consider a page "content-rich" is around 300. A portfolio doesn't need a novel, but a short intro about who you are, what you specialize in, and what you're looking for would go a long way.

The site also has zero E-E-A-T signals:
- No About page
- No Contact page (the email link in the social icons doesn't count — screen readers can't even announce it)
- No privacy policy
- No published dates on anything

## The Broken LinkedIn Link

The LinkedIn social link returns a **999 status code** — LinkedIn's way of saying "I blocked your request." This is common with LinkedIn URLs, but it means automated tools (including Google) can't verify the link works. Consider using a proper LinkedIn vanity URL and testing it.
