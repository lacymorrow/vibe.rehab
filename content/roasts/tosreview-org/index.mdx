# ToSReview.org: The ToS Tool Locked Behind Its Own Terms

**Submitted by:** Nithen
**The site:** tosreview.org
**Score: 31/100 - Grade F**

## First Impressions

Nithen submitted this with "no one is using it."

Our web crawler hit the site and found 38 characters of content. Total. Just the title tag. Empty page. I almost wrote this up as a blank landing page and moved on.

Then I opened it in a real browser. There's a legal disclaimer wall. "I Understand and Accept." I clicked it.

There's a whole product behind that wall. URL input. Paste-text analysis. One-click buttons for Google, Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, X, ChatGPT Terms of Service. Multi-language dropdown. An About page. A Privacy Policy. A roadmap.

Nithen built a real product and then hid it from the entire internet.

## The Irony

ToSReview analyzes Terms of Service agreements. The first thing it shows you is a Terms of Service agreement. You have to click "I Accept" before you can see anything.

The disclaimer renders client-side. No server-side fallback. Google's crawler doesn't click buttons. So Google sees a title tag and nothing else. Every social media preview is blank. Every SEO tool reports an empty site.

A Terms of Service analysis tool that no one can find because of its own Terms of Service. That's not a bug. That's poetry.

## What Google Sees

31/100. Here's why:

No sitemap. No robots.txt. No H1 tag. No meta description. No Open Graph tags. No `<main>` landmark. Zero words of indexable content.

Google doesn't know this site has pages. It doesn't know what the product does. It can't show a description in search results because there isn't one. Sharing the link on Twitter or LinkedIn shows nothing. Just a URL.

Performance is 96/100, but that's because an empty page loads fast.

## The Product

Once you're past the gate, it's good. Clean dark purple UI. The analysis tool is focused and functional. The quick-launch buttons for major platforms are a smart touch, since those are the Terms of Service most people care about. Multi-language support suggests ambition. Privacy Policy and About pages suggest someone thinking about the long game.

This isn't a template. Someone built this to solve a real problem. Billions of people click "I Accept" every day without reading what they're agreeing to. A tool that translates those documents into plain language has a huge potential audience.

The problem is that none of those people will ever find it.

## Fixes

1. **Kill the disclaimer gate.** Move the legal language to a banner, a footer, or a collapsible section. The content needs to be visible without interaction. This one fix changes everything.
2. **Add server-side rendering.** The homepage content needs to exist in the initial HTML response.
3. **Create a sitemap.xml and robots.txt.** 10-minute job.
4. **Write a meta description.** "Analyze any Terms of Service with AI. Paste a URL or text and understand what you're agreeing to." That works.
5. **Add Open Graph tags.** Title, description, image. Three meta tags.
6. **Add an H1 and heading structure.**
7. **Add a `<main>` landmark** for screen readers.
8. **Add internal links.** The homepage currently links to nothing.

**Time estimate:** 1-2 days. The gate restructure is most of the work.

## Verdict

31/100. A real product that chose to be invisible. The concept is strong. The execution behind the wall is solid. But none of that matters when Google can't see past the front door.

Fix the gate. Add basic SEO. Let the product speak for itself.

**Overall Score: 31/100 - Grade F**
