# ToSReview.org: The ToS Tool Locked Behind Its Own Terms

**Submitted by:** Nithen
**The site:** tosreview.org
**Score: 31/100 - Grade F**

## First Impressions

Nithen submitted this with the note "no one is using it" and I was ready to write another "empty landing page" roast. Then I actually clicked through the site.

Wait. Hold on.

There's a legal disclaimer you have to accept before you see anything. I clicked "I Understand and Accept" and suddenly... there's a whole product in here? A URL input, a paste-text option, quick-launch buttons for Google, Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, X, and ChatGPT's Terms of Service. A roadmap page. An About page. A Privacy Policy. Multi-language support.

This isn't a blank page. This is a real, functional tool that *chose to hide itself from the entire internet.*

## The Irony Is Almost Art

Let me explain what's happening. ToSReview is a tool that analyzes Terms of Service documents. And the first thing it does when you visit is show you... a Terms of Service wall. That you have to accept. Before you can see anything.

And because that wall is rendered client-side with no server-side fallback, every search engine, every social media preview, every web scraper, and every SEO audit tool sees the same thing: a title tag and an empty void. Our crawler found **38 characters of content**. That's the title.

A Terms of Service analysis tool that's invisible to the internet because of its own Terms of Service. You can't make this up.

## What Google Actually Sees

Our audit came back at **31/100 - Grade F.** Here's the damage:

- **No XML sitemap** - Google doesn't know what pages exist
- **No robots.txt** - not even the basic "hey crawlers, here's how to find stuff" file
- **No H1 tag** on the homepage - because the homepage is a disclaimer modal
- **No meta description** - Google has nothing to show in search results
- **No Open Graph tags** - sharing on social media shows a broken preview
- **Zero words of indexable content** - the entire product is behind a JavaScript wall
- **No `<main>` landmark** - screen readers can't find the main content either

The homepage scores 96/100 on performance though. Because serving nothing is extremely fast.

## The Actual Product (It's Not Bad!)

Once you get past the gate, ToSReview is a clean, focused tool. You can:

- Paste a URL to any Terms of Service document and get an AI-powered analysis
- Paste raw text directly for analysis
- One-click analyze major platforms (Google, Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, X, ChatGPT)
- The UI is clean and the dark purple gradient looks professional
- There's multi-language support via a dropdown
- They have an About page, Privacy Policy, and a Roadmap

This is not a half-baked side project. Someone actually built something useful. Then they hid it behind a gate that makes it impossible for anyone to discover.

## What's Actually Working

- **Performance: 96/100** - lightning fast (granted, there's almost nothing to load initially)
- **The product itself** - once you're in, it's functional and well-designed
- **The concept** - ToS analysis is a genuinely useful niche with massive potential
- **Security: 83/100** - HTTPS properly configured, decent security headers
- **They have a privacy policy** - the link just isn't on the gated homepage
- **Mobile: 100/100** - fully responsive

## The Fix List

Here's what I'd do, in order:

1. **Remove or restructure the disclaimer gate** - move it to a banner, a footer note, or a first-use modal that doesn't block crawlers. The disclaimer content can live on the page without being a wall. This single change fixes almost everything.
2. **Add server-side rendering for the homepage** - make sure Google can see your product even with the disclaimer.
3. **Add a sitemap.xml and robots.txt** - takes 10 minutes, lets Google know your site exists.
4. **Add a meta description** - "Analyze any Terms of Service with AI. Paste a URL or text and get a plain-English breakdown of what you're agreeing to." Done.
5. **Add OG tags** - so sharing on social media actually shows something.
6. **Add an H1 and proper heading structure** - basic but essential.
7. **Add a `<main>` landmark** - screen readers need to know where the content is.
8. **Add internal links** - the homepage currently has zero outgoing internal links according to our crawler.

**Estimated fix time:** 1-2 days. The disclaimer restructure is the big one, everything else is standard SEO hygiene.
**Priority:** Critical - the site literally does not exist to search engines right now

## Final Verdict

ToSReview is the most frustrating kind of audit because the product is *right there*. It works. It looks good. It solves a real problem. But Nithen put a bouncer at the door who won't let Google in, and now nobody knows the club exists.

The concept of analyzing Terms of Service is genuinely valuable - billions of people click "I Accept" without reading what they're agreeing to. A tool that breaks those agreements down in plain language could reach millions of users. The irony that this specific tool is inaccessible because of its own "I Accept" button is... honestly kind of poetic.

Fix the gate. Let Google in. Everything else is cleanup.
