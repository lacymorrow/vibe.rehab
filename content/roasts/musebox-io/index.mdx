# Musebox.io: A Prompt Library That Needs Its Own Prompt

**Submitted by:** Nigel Yoshida
**The site:** musebox.io
**Score: 54/100 - Grade F**

## First Impressions

Okay I'm not gonna lie, I was pleasantly surprised at first. Musebox actually looks like a real product. It's got features, users, a community page, blog, docs. Someone clearly put in serious work here. The homepage SEO metadata is solid - OG tags, Twitter cards, structured data, proper meta descriptions. The bones are real.

And then I started clicking around.

## The Login Wall Problem

So our crawler found 803 URLs in the sitemap. Eight hundred and three! That's impressive! Until you actually click on one.

I went to visit a prompt page and got: **"Login Required - To view 'Brainstorm Buddy' and other community prompts, please sign in to your account."** Every. Single. Prompt. Page. All 800+ of them.

So let me get this straight: you indexed 800+ URLs into Google, Google shows them in search results, someone clicks through excited to see a prompt, and they hit a login wall with zero content. That's not just bad for SEO - that's actively making people *not* want to sign up. You're training potential users to associate your brand with disappointment.

The pages that are publicly visible? Most have thin content - under 300 words. Some clock in at 55-65 words. That's a database row with a URL, not a page.

And the keyword stuffing makes it worse:
- "prompt" at 5.1% density on the homepage
- "your" at 3.6-5.5% across multiple pages
- "musebox" at 3.2% on the docs page

## Accessibility: Oof

54/100 overall, but accessibility is where this really hurts. 10 errors and 66 warnings across 25 pages.

The newsletter signup email field? No label. Screen readers have no idea what that input is for. There's also a honeypot field (`b_6f33b644bdbc5f30e2acc4004_d9c2c64a21`) sitting in the DOM without an accessible label - screen readers will try to announce it and confuse everyone.

No `<main>` landmark on the blog and features pages, so assistive tech can't find the main content. 5 videos across the homepage and features page with no captions - bad for accessibility *and* SEO. And 33 color contrast issues because that gray-on-gray dark mode aesthetic? Yeah, it fails WCAG for a lot of users.

## The Image Situation

Okay this one genuinely got me. You're using Next.js. The framework that is *literally famous* for image optimization. And somehow:

- One avatar image is **620KB** being served through `/_next/image?w=3840&q=75`
- You're requesting a **3840px wide version of a profile picture**
- 65 images are missing width/height attributes, causing layout shift on every prompt page
- 16 below-fold images on the community page aren't lazy loaded
- But the above-fold images on the features page ARE lazy loaded - which is the exact opposite of what you want

I just... how? You have the tool. It's right there. It's built into your framework. You just have to give it the right props.

## The Legal Gap

No privacy policy on 19 pages (all the prompt pages). The homepage has one in the footer, but Google crawls individual pages. A platform handling user data needs that footer on every single page.

No contact page either. If someone has a problem with their content being shared, how are they supposed to reach you?

The JSON-LD on the homepage also has an invalid `Organization.logo` - it's an object where it should be a string URL, so Google's rich results won't pick up your brand correctly.

## What's Actually Working

Real talk - Musebox has the most potential of anything we've roasted so far:

- It's a real product with real users and real features
- The homepage SEO metadata is actually solid
- The design is good - clean, professional, functional
- 803 indexed pages means Google knows you exist
- The community and documentation show real investment

This isn't a template someone deployed and forgot about. Someone built this with care. The website just hasn't kept up with the product.

## The Fix List

Here's what I'd do, in order:

1. **Make prompt pages publicly visible** - at least show the prompt title, description, and category without login. Gate the "copy" or "use" action, not the entire page. 800 indexed pages that show a login wall is actively harmful.
2. **Beef up those prompt pages** - add examples, use cases, related prompts. Get each page above 300 words, or noindex the truly thin ones.
3. **Fix accessibility** - label all form inputs, add `<main>` landmarks, add video captions, fix contrast ratios.
4. **Fix the images** - add proper `width`/`height`/`sizes` to all Image components. That 620KB avatar? Cap it at 200px wide.
5. **Add privacy policy to the global layout** - one change, fixes 19 pages.
6. **Fix the JSON-LD** - make `Organization.logo` a string URL.
7. **Add VideoObject schema** - those demo videos should be discoverable in Google Video search.

**Estimated fix time:** 2-3 weeks
**Priority:** High - the thin content and accessibility issues are actively hurting rankings and excluding users

## Final Verdict

Musebox is close. Like, genuinely close. The product is there, the users are there, the effort is clearly there. But the site is making classic mistakes - indexing hundreds of thin pages, neglecting accessibility, and somehow breaking image optimization on the framework that invented image optimization.

Nigel, you're closer than you think. The product is ahead of the website. Time to let the website catch up.

**Overall Score: 54/100 - Grade F** (but with a clear path to B+)
