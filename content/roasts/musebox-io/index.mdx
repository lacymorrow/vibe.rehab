# Musebox.io: A Prompt Library That Needs Its Own Prompt

**Submitted by:** Nigel Yoshida
**The site:** musebox.io
**Score: 54/100 - Grade F**

## First Impressions

The homepage is solid. Like, actually solid. Clean design, pricing tiers, community page, blog, docs, a Chrome extension. OG tags, Twitter cards, structured data. Somebody cared about this. You can feel the hours.

Then I clicked on a prompt.

## 800 Locked Doors

Musebox has 803 URLs in its sitemap. That's a lot of pages for Google to index! And Google did index them. But when a human clicks through from search results, every single prompt page shows a login wall. "Login Required." No preview, no description, no teaser. Just a padlock icon and two buttons.

So Google sends people to Musebox, and Musebox slams the door in their face. 800 times.

This is worse than thin content. Thin content is disappointing. A login wall is hostile. Someone searched "brainstorm buddy prompt," found your page, clicked it, and learned nothing except that your site doesn't want them there. That person is not signing up. That person is hitting the back button and never coming back.

The pages that *are* public have their own problems. Most land under 300 words. Some have 55. That's a tweet, not a page.

Keyword density is rough too. "Prompt" at 5.1% on the homepage. "Your" at 3.6-5.5% all over. The copy reads like someone told an LLM to "emphasize prompt engineering" and it took that very literally.

## Accessibility

10 errors. 66 warnings. Across 25 pages.

The newsletter email field has no label. A screen reader just encounters a mystery box. The honeypot field (`b_6f33b644bdbc5f30e2acc4004_d9c2c64a21`) doesn't have a label either, so screen readers announce it to users who shouldn't even know it exists.

Blog and features pages are missing `<main>` landmarks. Five videos with no captions. 33 color contrast failures across the site because the dark mode palette prioritized looking cool over being readable.

## The 620KB Profile Picture

Musebox runs on Next.js. Next.js has built-in image optimization. It is, famously, one of the main reasons people use Next.js.

One avatar on the community page loads at `w=3840&q=75`. That's a 3840-pixel-wide profile picture. 620KB. For a circle that renders at maybe 48 pixels on screen.

65 images are missing width/height attributes, so every prompt page has layout shift while images load. 16 below-fold images on the community page load eagerly. Above-fold images on the features page load lazily. That's backwards. Both of those are backwards.

## Legal Gaps

19 prompt pages have no privacy policy link. The homepage footer has one, but Google evaluates pages individually. A platform that handles user content needs that link everywhere.

No contact page. If someone finds their prompt shared without permission, there's no way to reach anyone.

The homepage JSON-LD has `Organization.logo` set to an object instead of a string URL. Google won't parse it. Small thing, easy fix.

## What Works

Musebox is the best product we've roasted. It's real. People use it. The community page has active contributors with badges. There's a Chrome extension. Documentation. A pricing page with a free tier. The homepage metadata is well-configured. This isn't a template or a weekend project.

The product outpaced the website somewhere along the way, and nobody circled back.

## Fixes

1. **Unlock the prompt pages.** Show the title, description, and category without login. Gate the copy/use action, not the page itself. This is the single highest-impact change.
2. **Bulk up or noindex thin pages.** Add examples, use cases, related prompts. If a page can't hit 300 words, noindex it.
3. **Label every form input.** Including the honeypot. Especially the honeypot.
4. **Cap avatar images at 200px wide.** Fix width/height on all 65 images. Flip lazy/eager loading.
5. **Add privacy policy link to the global layout.** One change, 19 pages fixed.
6. **Fix the JSON-LD logo.** String URL, not object.
7. **Add captions to all five videos.** And VideoObject schema while you're at it.

**Time estimate:** 2-3 weeks.

## Verdict

54/100. Grade F, but barely. This is a real product with a website that hasn't caught up to it. The login wall on prompt pages is actively driving away the traffic Google sends you. Fix that, fix the images, fix the accessibility, and you're looking at a B+.

Nigel, the product is ahead of the marketing. Close the gap.

**Overall Score: 54/100 - Grade F**
